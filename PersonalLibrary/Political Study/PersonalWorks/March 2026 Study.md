To begin with, microwaves are exactly the same thing as light. They're both electromagnetic radiation. The microwave waveband covers millimeters to centimeters in wavelength, but they are still electromagnetic radiation, and so as a result they behave very very similarly despite the wavelength difference. For example, when light leaves a low-density medium like air, enters a high-density medium like glass, and then re-enters a low-density medium like air, it can bend at those interfaces; it can be focused. 1. Decide on a backing material
	- This will be what supports the coating
	- Concrete, steel, glass, plaster, wood, even cardboard
		- This video uses a cardboard packing box
	- For this video, cardboard was cut to an active/blocking area of 1000cm^2 x 3mm
2. Decide on a resin system
	-  Silicone
		- High temperature tolerance, very flexible
	- Urethanes
		- UV resistant (good in sunlight), stronger than epoxy
		- Poor fatigue characteristics
	- Epoxy
		- Probably one of the better/more universal and accessible materials
		- Proper mixing ratios (in video, Reika is used at a 2:1 ;resin:hardener)
3. Mixing and Application
	- Measure epoxy resin into container, then add measured hardener
	- When adding materials to a resin system, always stir the resin/hardener mixture together neat first.
		- This ensures as even a distribution as possible of the additive materials
