> 
> - ### General Concept
>  I believe that a historical analysis of the many domestic and international policies this country has failed to implement properly and effectively will help the average American understand the fact that many of these failures of policy are not accidental, and to also understand the class interests which these failures of policy invariably serve.
>  
>  For instance, the failures of reparations in the wake of slavery and southern race law could be seen as an innocent, accidental failure of truthfully well-meaning but bumbling public servants; alternatively, it can be seen as a willful 'murder by neglect' of the potential of Black communities by forcing them to live in material want (just as the US also does to Cuba). This lack of proper reparative support to oppressed peoples, unsurprising as it is to see from the US Bourgeois Dictatorship, has created a purposefully-underdeveloped strata of the Proletariat and Lumpenproletariat which serve, at one and the same time, as working class and slave class, depending only on one's personal carceral status. The 13th Amendment of the Constitution of the United States states "*Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude, except as a punishment for crime whereof the party shall have been duly convicted, shall exist within the United States, or any place subject to their jurisdiction.*"; this is the same loophole which allowed the Clinton dynasty to staff their Arkansas mansion with slaves-on-lease from local prisons during the childhood of Hilary Rodham Clinton ("*using prison labor at the governor’s mansion was a longstanding tradition, which kept down costs.*").
>  
>  Through another lens, the lip-service paid to LGBTQIA+ communities in combination with the lack of real material support and societal education could be said to give the more "progressive" liberals in the US enough social cover to conceal the true end-point to their seemingly innocent failure: the community as a whole is pushed 'front and center' in the American Spectacle, but the reactionary elements of society were both allowed to remain uneducated on the history of the LGBTQIA+ community and, often by way of state-sponsored media, galvanized in their disdain for this very same community which their owners have instructed them to "other".
>  
>  To 'pull the camera back' a distance, the constant imperialist intervention in the SWANA (Southwest Asia, North Africa) region and the Levant have greatly deteriorated both the material developments and the social conditions within every affected country. These factors work in concert with continued US, NATO, IMF, and World Bank intervention in every corner of society to sustain a region rife with both conflict and natural resources; this is both a 'failure of policy' and a perfect opportunity for the chain of Multinational Investment and Military Protectionism Parenti spoke of in 'Face of Imperialism' to take hold.
>  
>  Similarly, Bill Clinton's "failure" to reconstruct the self-reliance in Haitian agriculture has gained him and his benefactors a (rephrase) fucking shitload of money.
>  
>  I need to put more personal study into the failures of policy in relation to Natural Disasters, Ruby Ridge, Waco, and other instances of failure-by-neglect which the average American conservative is familiar with. There certainly exist within the ranks of American conservative tradition those who have the kernel of class consciousness, but have otherwise been misguided by the American Spectacle; we must find them, speak to them in terms that they understand, and bring them alongside us in the fight for all of our freedom from exploitation.
